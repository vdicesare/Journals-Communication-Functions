# Operationalising journals' communication functions through citation patterns and local dimensions

Victoria Di Césare (1)*, Nicolas Robinson-Garcia (1), and Rodrigo Costas (2)

(1) Unit for Computational Humanities and Social Sciences (U-CHASS), University of Granada, Spain

(2) Centre for Science and Technology Studies (CWTS), Leiden University, The Netherlands; DSI-NRF Centre of Excellence in Scientometrics and
Science, Technology and Innovation Policy (SciSTIP), Stellenbosch University, South Africa; Department of Science and Technology Policy, State
University of Campinas, Brazil

*Corresponding author: vdicesare@ugr.es

Abstract: This preliminary study operationalises the knowledge bridging and gap-filling communication functions of scientific journals,
originally proposed by Chavarro et al. (2017). Considering their theoretical definitions, we link these functions to our local research
framework (Di Césare & Robinson-Garcia, 2026) through the producers and recipients dimensions, and measure them using local or global
references and citations drawn from OpenAlex articles. We aim to empirically identify knowledge bridging and gap-filling journals, both
within and outside the mainstream. The results suggest that the vast majority of these journals are non-mainstream. The thematic profiles
of their references and citations validate our geographical operationalisations. Gap-filling journals belong predominantly to the
non-mainstream local Social Sciences, whereas in the non-mainstream knowledge bridging journals, the Health Sciences emerge. These
domain-level findings support the journals’ theoretical functions of serving, respectively, as venues for the publication of knowledge
overlooked by the mainstream, and as bridges between mainstream knowledge and local communities.
